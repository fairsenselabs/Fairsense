01 · The White Space
Why the gap exists — and why it closes

BioCatch owns behavioral biometrics. Chainalysis owns on-chain forensics. Sardine owns device fingerprinting. None of them made the architectural decision early enough to score both Web2 behavioral signals and Web3 on-chain signals inside a single verdict engine. That gap is WalletSense's entire right to exist.

The incumbents are domain-native. Their data models, infrastructure, and sales motion reflect their founding lane. Merging those stacks now means breaking existing enterprise contracts. It's the innovator's dilemma — their depth is their constraint.

The hybrid attack patterns that exploit this gap are real and growing: a human passes biometric onboarding (invisible to BioCatch), then hands the session to an automation script at signing. Behavioral tools see a clean session. On-chain tools see a clean wallet. Neither sees the handoff. WalletSense does.

07 · Pre-GTM Requirements
What must be true before the first serious sales conversation
Benchmark report
Every technical buyer will run WalletSense against a labeled dataset — known bots vs. known humans. If false positive rate on legitimate human traffic exceeds ~3%, the product is dead regardless of true positive performance. BioCatch publishes theirs. Publish yours first.
Legal definition of "confirmed fraud"
The Bot Bounty model lives or dies on this definition. Write it into every pilot contract before deployment. What counts as confirmed? Who adjudicates disputes? Without this, the performance fee model becomes a legal argument.
MAS/PDPA compliance posture
Behavioral biometrics at 1,000Hz generates biometric data under PDPA. Singapore clients need a clear DPA, data residency answer, and retention policy before procurement will sign. This is not optional — it's a blocker for every regulated institution.
Language audit on all materials
"Mathematically final proof" and "laws of physics" will be challenged by any technical due diligence team. Strip the marketing superlatives. Replace with tested accuracy metrics and confidence intervals. The underlying capability is strong — it doesn't need amplification.
